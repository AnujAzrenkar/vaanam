The idea

Not a resort built beside nature. A farm you are invited into.

Dharbandora sits in Goa's quiet east, where the plains give way to the Western Ghats and the Mhadei river begins its run. There are no beaches here — there are leopard corridors, betel groves, and mornings that smell of wet laterite and cardamom.

Vaanam is forty acres of that. We farm pepper, nutmeg and areca the way the land asks for, and we kept just eight cottages so the place never fills with noise.

A day, roughly

We don't schedule your stay.
But this is how the hours tend to go.

i.

Dawn on the river

Coffee carried to your verandah before the mist lifts. The Mhadei runs cold and clear past the lower field — most guests walk to it barefoot.

ii.

Walk the spice rows

Our farmer Mahesh will show you where the pepper climbs and the nutmeg falls. You'll leave knowing how a single vine perfumes an entire acre.

iii.

Lunch from the fire

Goan-Saraswat cooking over open flame — kokum, coconut, and whatever the morning's foraging brought back. Long tables, slow plates.

iv.

The sanctuary at dusk

A guided amble to the Mhadei boundary, when the hornbills cross and the forest gets loud. Back before dark, by lantern light.

Why eco, honestly

The farm came first. We just made room for you.

Everything here runs on what the land already gives. Solar through the dry months, spring-fed water, greywater back to the groves, and a kitchen that buys nothing it can grow. It isn't a marketing line — it's the only way forty acres of forest stays forty acres of forest.
